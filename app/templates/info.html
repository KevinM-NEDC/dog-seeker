How it was Made

The Development of Dog Seeker was made over 3 weeks, by us, the Screaming Sirens. It became increasingly trial and error-esque near the end, but we persevered.

Step 1: Brainstorming

During week 1 of AI camp, we befriended one another, and got to work on learning the basics of JavaScript and Python. We began to brainstorm what our AI would do, and we came up with some great ideas: an AI that detects landmarks, Waldo from Where's Waldo, or any of the given prompts from a ReCaptcha. In the end, we had decided to create an AI that specifies the breed of a dog, and began the next step.

Step 2: Gathering and Labeling Data

Once we decided on our AI, we began to code on a server to collect hundreds of images of dogs using SerpAPI. We uploaded these images to Roboflow, where we labeled the dogs from each image by their breed. This would serve as training, validation, and testing for our AI.

Step 3: Training the AI

After labeling our images on RoboFlow, we created a Google Colab project to train the AI using YOLOv5. We went through several iterations of our dataset, until we finalized our dataset on our fourth trial: a total of around 6000 images, from the 13 dog breeds we were using and the augmentations we used to distort the original images (such as mosaic and crop). The confusion matrix from this trial made clear that this training was the most successful and would be our final product.

Step 4: Designing the Website

After completing the AI, we began designing a website to host our AI. We learned the basics of HTML and CSS and used our knowledge to work from templates we found on BootStrap. Although they were originally templates, we used our creativity to make these website truly ours. This step was where he most bugs began to pop up, which stumped us many, many times. We persevered however, and found solutions to most, if not all of them.
